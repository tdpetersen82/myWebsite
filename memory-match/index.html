How to play Memory Match

Memory Match is the classic card-flipping memory game, also known as Concentration. Tap a card to flip it, then tap another — if they match, they stay; if not, they flip back. Find every pair on the board to win. Three difficulty sizes from 12 cards up to 80.

Controls

  • Tap or click a card — flip it face-up
  • Tap a second card — if it matches, both stay
  • Easy Medium Hard — pick the board size
  • New game — shuffle and reset

Tips & strategy

  • Sweep the rows. First few flips, scan each card in order. Memorizing positions early makes later matches easy.
  • Match with intent. When you see a card you've seen before, flip its known partner first — don't gamble on the second flip.
  • Track misses, not hits. Pairs you've matched are gone. Your job is remembering where the unmatched cards are.
  • Work corners outward. On bigger boards, lock down the corner positions first — they're easier to keep straight in your head.

About Memory Match

Memory Match is the classic Concentration card game, recorded in family game collections going back to the early 20th century. The mechanic — flip two, match or flip back — is a foundational kids' memory exercise. The Hard 80-card board is genuinely hard, even for adults.
