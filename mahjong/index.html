How to play Mahjong Solitaire online

Mahjong Solitaire is the classic tile-matching puzzle: clear the whole layout by removing matching pairs of free tiles. It's a calm, one-player game of pattern-spotting and planning — no dice, no opponent, just you and the stack. This free browser version offers three layouts from Easy to Hard, with no downloads or signup.

Mahjong Solitaire rules

  • Match pairs. Remove tiles two at a time by picking two identical tiles — same suit and number, or a matching pair from the flower and season groups.
  • Only free tiles. A tile is “free” when nothing is stacked on top of it and at least one side — left or right — is open. Blocked tiles can't be played until you clear what traps them.
  • Clear the board. Keep matching free pairs until every tile is gone. That's the win.
  • Stuck? If no free matches remain, the layout is jammed — start over or try a shuffle if available.

Mahjong Solitaire tips & strategy

  • Free the stacks first. Prioritize matches that unlock tall piles and long rows — they open the most new moves.
  • Look before you match. When four of a kind are showing, choose the pair that frees the most blocked tiles.
  • Don't strip one side. Clearing tiles evenly keeps more tiles “free” on both edges.
  • Plan around key tiles. The tiles bridging two halves of the layout are precious — don't waste their matches early.
  • Think a few moves ahead. Every removal changes what's free next; the win is in the sequencing, not the speed.

Frequently asked

How do I play Mahjong Solitaire? Click two identical free tiles to remove them. A tile is free if it has no tile on top and an open left or right edge. Clear every tile to win.

Is this the same as four-player Mahjong? No — this is Mahjong Solitaire, the single-player tile-matching puzzle (sometimes called Shanghai or Taipei). It uses the same beautiful tiles but is played solo.

Why can't I match two tiles? One of them is blocked — a tile must be clear on top and open on the left or right to be playable. Free the tile trapping it first.
