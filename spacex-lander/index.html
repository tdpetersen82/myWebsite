How to play SpaceX Lander

SpaceX Lander is a modern take on the classic Lunar Lander, with realistic Falcon-9-style booster physics. You're flying a tall, top-heavy rocket against gravity, wind, and a thin fuel budget — aim for a vertical touchdown on the drone-ship pad. Slow, level, and on-target wins.

Controls

  • ↑ — main engine throttle
  • ← → — gimbal / cold-gas thrusters
  • R — restart

Tips & strategy

  • Throttle, don't toggle. The booster behaves like a real rocket — sudden full bursts will overcorrect. Smooth, modulated thrust is the entire game.
  • Watch the vertical. Speed at touchdown is what kills the landing. Bleed off velocity high up; don't suicide-burn at the last second.
  • Stay upright. A tall booster falls over fast. Keep your tilt close to vertical, especially as you approach the pad.
  • Wind drift matters. Crosswinds push the booster sideways. Anticipate the drift and aim slightly upwind of the pad.

About SpaceX Lander

SpaceX Lander is a tribute to the engineering that made vertical-landing reusable boosters real. SpaceX's Falcon 9 first stage flew back from orbit and landed itself on a drone ship for the first time in April 2016 (Of Course I Still Love You, in the Atlantic) — an outcome that decades of aerospace engineers had assumed was prohibitively expensive. This game uses physics inspired by that flight envelope.

Frequently asked

How do I play SpaceX Lander? Use the up arrow to throttle the main engine and the left/right arrows to gimbal. Touch down vertically on the landing pad without crashing or running out of fuel.

Is SpaceX Lander free? Yes — no signup, no downloads, no installs.

How is this different from Lunar Lander? SpaceX Lander models a tall, slender booster with gimbal-thrust steering. Lunar Lander uses a more forgiving, low-CG capsule. Both reward gentle throttle.

Does my high score save? Yes — your best landing score is stored locally in your browser.
