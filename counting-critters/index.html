How to play Counting Critters

Counting Critters is a friendly numeracy game for kids ages 4 to 10. Each round shows you a group of bobbing animals; tap the number that matches how many you see. Twenty questions per round, no timer, and the counts get a little harder as you go.

Controls

  • Tap or click — pick a number
  • New round — start over

Tips & strategy

  • Count in pairs. Easier than counting one by one — group the critters into twos or fives and add.
  • Take your time. There's no clock. Slow, accurate counting beats fast guesses.
  • Read the level. Early questions use small numbers; later questions can go up to 10. The numeric range on the answer buttons tells you what to expect.
  • Mix up the critters. Once you've nailed counting one species, the game mixes types — count them all, not just one kind.

About Counting Critters

Counting Critters is a kindergarten-through-second-grade numeracy game in the tradition of classic counting workbooks — just turned interactive. Critters bob with cheerful animations, answers light up green for correct, soft pink for wrong, and the only feedback is encouraging.
