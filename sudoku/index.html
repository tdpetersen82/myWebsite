How to play Sudoku

Sudoku is a logic puzzle played on a 9×9 grid split into nine 3×3 boxes. Some cells start with clue numbers. Your job is to fill in the rest so that every row, every column, and every 3×3 box contains the digits 1 through 9 exactly once. There is no arithmetic and no guessing — every puzzle here has a single solution reachable by logic alone.

Controls

  • Click or tap a cell to select it, then click a number on the pad — or press 1–9.
  • ← → ↑ ↓ — move the selection around the grid.
  • Backspace / Delete — erase the selected cell.
  • N — toggle pencil-note mode; H — reveal one correct cell; Ctrl+Z — undo.

Tips & strategy

  • Hunt the singles. Scan for a cell where only one digit can legally fit, or a box where a digit can go in only one spot. Fill those first.
  • Use pencil notes. When a cell could be two or three values, jot them down. As you place numbers elsewhere, candidates shrink until one is forced.
  • Work the dense areas. Rows, columns, and boxes that are already mostly full constrain their empty cells the hardest — they crack first.
  • Tap a number to highlight it. Selecting a filled cell shades every matching digit, making it easy to spot where that number still needs to go.

About the difficulties

Every puzzle is generated fresh and verified to have exactly one solution. Easy and Medium fall out from straightforward scanning. Hard and Expert leave fewer clues and demand candidate tracking and chains of deduction. Your fastest clean solve for each level is saved in your browser, along with any puzzle you leave unfinished.
