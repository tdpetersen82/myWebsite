How to play Connect 4

Connect 4 is the two-player classic where you take turns dropping discs into a 7-column, 6-row grid. Discs fall to the lowest open slot in the column you pick. The first player to line up four of their own discs in a row — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally — wins. If the grid fills with no four-in-a-row, it's a draw.

Controls

  • Click or tap a column — drop your disc into the lowest open slot
  • New game — clear the grid and start over

Difficulty

  • Easy — shallow look-ahead and the occasional random drop; forgiving.
  • Medium — 4-ply minimax; blocks your threats and builds its own.
  • Hard — 6-ply minimax with alpha-beta pruning and center weighting.

Tips & strategy

  • Own the center column. It runs through more winning lines than any other column — take it early.
  • Make double threats. Set up two ways to win at once so a single block can't stop both.
  • Watch the odd rows. As the first player, threats that complete on odd rows (1, 3, 5 from the bottom) are especially strong.
  • Block before you build. If the computer has three in a row with an open end, stop it before chasing your own line.

About Connect 4

Connect Four was published by Milton Bradley in 1974. It's a "solved" game: with perfect play the first player can always force a win by starting in the center column. The grid here is the standard 7×6.
